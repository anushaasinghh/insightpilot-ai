# User Research — InsightPilot AI

## Research Goals
Understand how non-technical business users currently analyse data,
what their biggest pain points are, and what would make them trust an AI tool.

## Research Methods
- 6 user interviews (30 min each, remote)
- Screener: business users who work with data but don't code
- Roles: marketing managers, sales leads, operations analysts, small business owners

## Key Findings

### Finding 1 — The "Excel Trap"
6/6 users rely on Excel/Google Sheets for all data analysis.
They know it's limiting but don't know what to use instead.
> "I spend 3 hours every Monday making the same pivot table. 
>  There has to be a better way." — Operations Analyst, fintech startup

### Finding 2 — Fear of AI Errors
Users are excited by AI but deeply afraid of it giving wrong numbers.
Trust is the #1 barrier to adoption.
> "I'd use it if I could verify the answers. 
>  I can't show my CEO a chart I don't understand." — Marketing Manager, SaaS

### Finding 3 — PDF Reports Are Currency
Every user said they need to share findings with someone else.
Email, Slack, and presentations are the delivery mechanism.
A downloadable report was rated "very important" by 5/6 users.

### Finding 4 — Setup Friction Kills Adoption
Users abandoned tools that required more than 2 steps to get started.
"Upload and go" is the expected model (Canva, Notion, Figma all do this).

## User Personas

### Persona 1 — "Dashboard-Dependent Divya"
- Role: Marketing Manager, 4 years experience
- Data tools: Excel, Google Sheets, occasional Looker
- Goal: Understand campaign performance without waiting for the data team
- Pain: "I raise a data request, wait 3 days, get a chart I didn't want"
- Key need: Self-serve insights in under 5 minutes

### Persona 2 — "Reporting-Overwhelmed Rohan"
- Role: Operations Analyst at a 50-person startup
- Data tools: Excel, Airtable, Google Data Studio
- Goal: Produce weekly ops report for leadership without it taking half his day
- Pain: Same report, different numbers, every single week — manual and error-prone
- Key need: Automated report generation with AI narrative

### Persona 3 — "Growth-Focused Giulia"
- Role: Head of Growth, e-commerce brand
- Data tools: Shopify analytics, Excel, sometimes Python (basic)
- Goal: Find what's driving revenue and act fast
- Pain: Has data but no time to analyse it properly
- Key need: Instant "so what" and not just charts, but conclusions
